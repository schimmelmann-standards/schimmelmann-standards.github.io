How do I store organic materials?

All organic materials are chemically metastable in the presence of elemental oxygen. It is generally recommended to place organic reference materials in dry and dark storage in a refrigerator or even freezer. Upon retrieval, please let the closed containers gently warm to room temperature before opening, in order to avoid condensation and entrapment of moisture. Reference materials with USGS numbers have designated shelf lives. In general, the end user should apply common sense when deciding on shelf lives in laboratories. To mention some extreme examples, n-alkanes in anoxic source rocks containing hydrocarbons are geochemically stable over millions of years, whereas hydrocarbons exposed to UV radiation and oxygen can degrade fast. Food matrix reference materials can get "stale" during inappropriate storage and thus possibly suffer isotopic fractionation, at least on a compound-specific level.
